# LinkedIn Post Templates — Yohann Doillon

**Voice rules:** Direct. Precise. Grounded.
- State the conclusion first, then the reasoning
- Short sentences for sharp points
- No buzzword soup. No fake enthusiasm. No blog-post-disguised-as-sales.
- Observation → insight → implication
- Max 3 bullets, or none at all

---

## Template 1: Insight Post

**What it is:** A sharp observation from real work. One thing you noticed that most people get wrong.

**Structure:**
```
[Counterintuitive statement or blunt observation — 1 sentence]

[The context that makes this surprising — 2-3 sentences]

[What this actually means / the implication — 1-2 sentences]

[Optional: 1 closing line that invites reflection, not engagement-bait]
```

**Example (Palantir / enterprise AI pillar):**
```
Most Palantir deployments fail before the first line of code is written.

The failure happens in the scoping conversation. Teams define success as
"get the platform live" — not "change how decisions get made." So they
optimize for deployment, not for outcomes. The platform goes live.
Nothing changes.

The fix isn't technical. It's agreeing on what operational leverage
actually looks like before the contract is signed.

This is what we spend most of our time on at 10x Partners. Not implementation.
The conversation before implementation.
```

**Usage notes:**
- Best for pillar 1 (Enterprise AI Deployment) or pillar 3 (GTM in Enterprise Tech)
- Post on Monday or Tuesday morning
- No hashtags unless genuinely relevant (max 2)
- No "What do you think?" at the end

## Template 2: Behind-the-Scenes Post

**What it is:** A specific moment, decision, or observation from real work. Not polished. Not a case study.

**Structure:**
```
[Scene-setter — what was happening, 1-2 sentences. Be specific.]

[The thing that happened / decision that was made — 2-3 sentences]

[Why it mattered / what you learned — 1-2 sentences]

[Optional: one honest take that doesn't resolve neatly]
```

**Example (Building at 10x pillar):**
```
Had a call last week where a client's CTO asked us to accelerate the timeline
by 6 weeks.

We said no.

Not because we couldn't move fast. Because the data model wasn't stable yet,
and rushing an AIP deployment onto a shifting ontology means you rebuild it
twice. We've seen that movie. It costs more time than the 6 weeks would have saved.

They pushed back. We held the line. The timeline stayed.

Sometimes the most valuable thing a partner does is slow you down in week 3
so you don't lose month 6.
```

**Usage notes:**
- Best for pillar 5 (Building at 10x) or pillar 4 (Operational Intelligence)
- Works best mid-week (Wednesday)
- Details make this format — specific > vague every time
- Don't clean it up too much. The rougher edge is the point.

## Template 3: Framework Post

**What it is:** A structured way of thinking about a specific problem. Not a listicle. A diagnostic.

**Structure:**
```
[The problem this framework solves — 1 sentence]

[The framework itself — 3 distinct elements, each 1-2 lines]

1. [Element]
2. [Element]
3. [Element]

[When to use it / what breaks it — 2-3 sentences]

[Optional: where this framework came from in your own work]
```

**Example (Enterprise AI Deployment pillar):**
```
Three reasons enterprise AI deployments stall — and only one of them is
actually a technology problem.

1. Data readiness. The ontology doesn't reflect how the business actually
   operates. You're automating the wrong thing.

2. Adoption architecture. The platform works. Nobody uses it. The rollout
   treated training as an afterthought, not a design constraint.

3. Value narrative. Leadership can't explain to the board what success looks
   like in dollar terms. The project loses budget in the next planning cycle.

The first one is technical. Two and three are GTM failures.

Most implementation partners are only equipped to solve number one.
```

**Usage notes:**
- Best for pillar 1, 2, or 3
- Post Thursday or Friday
- Number the framework elements — it aids scanning
- End with a sharp observation, not a question
- Works well with a pull quote in the image (optional)

## Cross-Template Rules

**Formatting:**
- Keep posts under 250 words where possible
- Use line breaks generously — LinkedIn collapses dense text
- No subheadings inside posts
- Bold sparingly — only for the most important phrase per post

**Cadence:**
- 2-3 posts per week maximum
- Rotate through templates and pillars
- Don't post two framework posts in a row

**What to avoid:**
- "I'm excited to share..."
- "This is a game-changer"
- "Thoughts?" at the end
- Tagging people just for reach
- Posts that are secretly sales pitches
