I grew up in Burgundy, France. Small city. Good wine. Long winters that leave a lot of time to think.

I left early — first for university, then for work, then for somewhere else entirely. That pattern repeated until Zurich, which is where I ended up and where I've stayed. I like it here. The city is serious without being cold. It suits the kind of work I want to do.

## What I do

I'm the CCO at [10x Partners](https://10x.partners), an official Palantir partner. We help organizations build and operate world-class data platforms — the kind that actually change how companies make decisions, not just the kind that look good in demos.

Most of my work sits at the uncomfortable intersection between technology and the humans who are supposed to use it. The data engineering is often the easy part. Getting an organization to change how it works — that's the hard part. That's where I spend most of my time.

Before 10x, I spent years moving between commercial and technical roles. I can read code and I can close deals, but what I care about is the layer in between: the operational decisions that determine whether a technology actually changes anything.

## What drives me

I'm drawn to hard problems in high-stakes environments. Not because I like suffering — because that's where real understanding lives. When something has to work, you can't fake it. You learn what matters and what doesn't.

Palantir figured this out before most people. They built for the customer no one else wanted to serve: intelligence agencies, defense contractors, organizations where failure is not acceptable. That forced rigor. It produced software that's actually used.

That's the model I care about. Not AI that demos beautifully. AI that changes how work gets done.

## Why I write

I write to think. Not to market myself. Not to build a personal brand.

When I work through a hard problem in writing, I understand it better. Sometimes the writing reveals that I was wrong. That's useful. The alternative — staying wrong without knowing it — is not.

This site is where that thinking happens in public. Everything here is a work in progress.
