Building in public has a genre.

Founder posts the loss. Gets 10,000 likes. Posts the win. Gets 50,000. The vulnerability is real but also polished. The lessons are packaged for the algorithm. Everyone claps for the journey.

I've been uncomfortable with that genre for a long time. And I've spent a while trying to figure out why — and whether that discomfort means I shouldn't write publicly at all.

## The problem with performance

The problem isn't authenticity. Most people who build in public are genuinely sharing real experiences.

The problem is selection. You share the story when you know the ending. The loss post is published after the recovery. The pivot post comes with the narrative of why it was actually the right move. Even real vulnerability is retrospective, shaped by knowing it worked out.

That selection creates a subtle distortion. It looks like transparent thinking, but it's actually pattern-matched storytelling. Successful outcome → identify the right lesson → package it for readers.

I don't think that's dishonest. I think it's just not what I'm interested in writing.

## What I'm actually trying to do

I work on things I can't fully disclose. Client engagements, strategic decisions, commercial negotiations — there's a real constraint on how much I can share in real time.

What I *can* share is how I think about problems. The frameworks I find durable. The patterns I've seen across deployments. The mistakes I've watched smart teams make repeatedly, including my own.

None of that requires a hero narrative. None of it requires you to wait for me to tell you how it ended.

It does require precision. Saying "here's what I actually believe, and here's why" rather than "here's the lesson from this experience." Showing the reasoning, not just the conclusion.

## Why this website

This site is my attempt to build a writing practice that's honest about what I know and honest about what I don't.

No performance of certainty. No lesson-packaging. Just thinking out loud, in public, in a domain I've spent serious time in.

If that's useful to you: welcome. If you're looking for the inspirational founder arc, I'm not the right writer for you — and I'd rather tell you that upfront than waste your time.

The posts here are going to be uneven. Some will be half-formed. Some will be wrong. That's the point.
